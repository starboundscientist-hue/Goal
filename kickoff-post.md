## The Method

**Build first, read papers second.** Implement the naive version. Feel the pain. Then read the paper that fixes it. The theory sticks when you've felt the problem.

**One artifact per thing.** No topic is done until there's code in a repo you can point to.

**AI accelerates but does not replace.** I use it to debug, explain, and articulate. I don't use it to write the code or design the solution. If I can't reproduce it without AI, I don't own it.

## Why This Exists

I don't have a famous name, a research lab, or an affiliation. I have a laptop and 25 focused hours a week. The embodied / space / autonomy stack has no single curriculum — the information is scattered across textbooks, papers, and blog posts. This is my attempt to collect the signal, skip the noise, and leave a trail for anyone who wants to move faster than I did.

This is my record of wanting it — and hopefully achieving everything a common person could dream of.
